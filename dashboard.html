Modelling Techniques

6 models — from classical to deep learning

📏 Simple Moving Average #6

Forecast = mean(last 12 values)

Averages the last 12 months. Gives a flat prediction — can't capture seasonality. Baseline only.

📈 Exponential Smoothing #5

F(t) = α·Y(t-1) + (1-α)·F(t-1)

Weighted average with more weight on recent values. α=1.0 means it just copies last month. No seasonal awareness.

🌡️ Holt-Winters #2

F = Level(α) + Trend(β) + Season(γ, s=12)

Triple exponential smoothing. Captures level, trend AND 12-month seasonal cycle. γ≈0.03 means season is very stable.

⭐ SARIMA #1 BEST

SARIMA(0,1,2)(1,1,1)[12] — AIC optimized

Seasonal ARIMA — the gold standard. AR + differencing + MA at both regular and seasonal levels. Grid search over 72+ combinations.

🔮 Facebook Prophet #3

y(t) = trend(t) + season(t) + ε(t)

Automatic decomposition. Piecewise linear trend + Fourier seasonality. Zero manual tuning needed. Great for non-experts.

🧠 LSTM Neural Network #4

LSTM(64) → LSTM(32) → Dense(32) → Dense(1)

Deep learning with memory gates (forget, input, output). Can learn non-linear patterns. 12-lag input, 50 epochs, walk-forward prediction.

🎓 Project Conclusions

  • Karnataka's rainfall is strongly seasonal — monsoon (Jun-Sep) contributes 66-88% of annual rainfall
  • SARIMA and Holt-Winters are the best models (R² > 0.78), reducing error by 70-75% over simple models
  • Simple models (SMA, SES) completely fail — they can't capture seasonality and fail the Ljung-Box test
  • LSTM offers no meaningful improvement over SARIMA for this regular, linear seasonal pattern
  • All forecasts predict near-normal rainfall with normal monsoon onset — no drought risk
  • Coastal KA shows significant climate change signal (+3.84 mm/year, p=0.004)
